ScotGEM funding arrangements

Applications have now closed

Applications for 2020 entry for this programme have now closed. For enquiries about undergraduate admissions for this course, please email medical.admissions@st-andrews.ac.uk. 

The information below explains the way in which your tuition fees and funding will be provided for all years of your Scottish Graduate Entry Medicine (ScotGEM) degree. 

The following arrangements are for the academic year 2019-2020. Guidance and legislation may change in future years. The most up-to-date information can be found on the NHS Student Bursaries web page.

  • Those with Home (Scotland or EU) fee status will have their tuition fees paid by the Scottish Government and will not need to apply to SAAS (Student Awards Agency Scotland) for payment of tuition fees.

    You may be eligible to apply for a means-tested maintenance loan from SAAS for the duration of the course (EU students are only eligible for tuition fee support).

    A ScotGEM ‘return of service’ bursary is available for up to four years of funding.

  • Year 1

    For the first year, students with RUK (England) fee status pay £3,645 and can apply to Student Finance England for the remainder of the tuition fee. 

    Students can also apply for a means-tested maintenance loan and supplementary grants. 

    Years 2 to 4

    For years 2 to 4, the NHS pays £3,715 and the student can apply to Student Finance England for the remainder of the tuition fee. 

    Students are eligible for the reduced rate maintenance loan along with a means-tested NHS bursary and a grant.

    A ScotGEM ‘return of service’ bursary is available for up to four years of funding.

  • Year 1

    For the first year, students with RUK (Wales) fee status pay £3,645 and can apply to Student Finance Wales for the remainder of the tuition fee. 

    Students can also apply for a means-tested maintenance loan and supplementary grants. 

    Years 2 to 4

    For years 2 to 4, the NHS pays £3,645 and the student can apply to Student Finance Wales for the remainder of the tuition fee. 

    Students are eligible for the reduced rate maintenance loan along with a means-tested NHS bursary and a grant.

    A ScotGEM ‘return of service’ bursary is available for up to four years of funding.

  • There is no tuition fee support for students with an RUK (Northern Ireland) fee status.

    Students are able to apply for a means-tested maintenance loan and supplementary grants.

    A ScotGEM ‘return of service’ bursary is available for up to four years of funding.

  • Students from the Channel Islands or the Isle of Man are advised to contact their island authority directly to determine if they are eligible for funding.

    There is no NHS tuition fee or bursary support for Islands students.

    A ScotGEM ‘return of service’ bursary is available for up to four years of funding.
