Partner medical schools

Applications have now closed

Applications for 2020 entry for this programme have now closed. For enquiries about undergraduate admissions for this course, please email medical.admissions@st-andrews.ac.uk. 

The School of Medicine has partnerships with six medical schools across the UK. Students on the Medicine BSc (Hons) A100 programme will progress to one of these schools upon graduating from St Andrews to undertake their final three years of training and graduate with a Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (MBChB/MBBS).

Students will be allocated depending on their chosen course pathway, location preference and academic performance in first semester of second year at St Andrews.

Prior to entry to the course at St Andrews, prospective students should consider the different medical school options available for their final years of study.

Scottish universities

  • The University of Aberdeen's School of Medicine and Dentistry is co-located on one of the largest clinical sites in Europe with extensive anatomy, clinical skills, and research facilities.

    Students will have the opportunity to experience both inner-city medicine and healthcare in remote and rural areas. Aberdeen offers clinical placements at Dr Gray's Hospital in Elgin and Raigmore Hospital in Inverness, and in locations including the Highlands and Islands, Fort William, Stornoway, Wick, Orkney and Shetland. 

    Students from St Andrews can join Year 3 of the Aberdeen MBChB and take advantage of a bridging course to help you integrate into the curriculum.

    Find out about tuition fees at the University of Aberdeen.

  • The University of Dundee's School of Medicine is fully integrated with Ninewells Teaching Hospital, offering an up-to-date and well-equipped clinical training facility very close to St Andrews. 

    Students will also gain experience in attachments to hospitals and general practices in the wider Tayside region, as well as other centres around Scotland and the UK.

    Students from St Andrews can join Year 3 of the Dundee MBChB.

    Find out more about tuition fees for the University of Dundee.

  • The Edinburgh Medical School, University of Edinburgh (College of Medicine and Veterinary Medicine), offers a six-year medical degree.

    The degree aims to prepare students for medical practice as a Foundation Year 1 doctor, and courses are designed to develop the knowledge and understanding, the skills and competencies, and the attitudes and attributes required by the profession.

    Students from St Andrews can join Year 4 of the Edinburgh MBChB.

    Find out about tuition fees for the University of Edinburgh.

  • The University of Glasgow's School of Medicine is one of the largest undergraduate schools in the UK. At Glasgow, you will have the opportunity to experience a diverse mixture of clinical attachments that range from national tertiary referral services to single-handed rural general practice.

    Students from St Andrews can join Year 3 of the Glasgow MBChB.

    Find out about tuition fees for the University of Glasgow.

English universities

  • The Queen Mary University of London's Barts and The London School of Medicine and Dentistry brings together two venerable teaching institutions: St Bartholomew’s Hospital (dating back to 1123) and The London Hospital Medical College (founded in 1785). The hospitals lie in two very different parts of London, the City and the East End, exposing you to a greater diversity of people and their health problems.

    The programme places considerable emphasis on developing your expertise in a whole range of practical areas, including clinical, communication, observation, team work and management skills.

    Students from St Andrews can join Year 3 of the London MBBS.

    Find out about tuition fees for Queen Mary University of London.

  • The University of Manchester's Medical School is the largest medical school in the UK. It has an international reputation for excellence in teaching and research, including close links with the Manchester Academic Health Science Centre, whole body cadavers used in anatomy, and the flexibility given to students to create a bespoke education.

    Manchester Medical School has a dedicated St Andrews coordinator, as well as peer mentors and academic advisors, to support your transition from St Andrews to Manchester.

    Students from St Andrews can join Year 3 of the Manchester MBChB.

    Find out about tuition fees for the University of Manchester.
