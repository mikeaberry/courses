How ScotGEM students are selected

Applications for 2021 entry are now closed

Applications for the 2021 entry cycle are closed. See courses for the upcoming academic year on the medicine subject page. 

The selection process for applicants to the Scottish Graduate Entry Medicine programme (ScotGEM) includes an assessment of all information on the application form including:

  • academic performance and predicted grades
  • personal statement and reference
  • aptitude test - the Graduate Medical School Admissions Test (GAMSAT)

There will be a further assessment by interview for a limited number of applicants. To be considered for interview, applicants must meet the following criteria:

  • submitted a full and complete UCAS application by the application deadline
  • meets or is predicted to meet the entry requirements
  • has a positive relevant reference
  • completed the online questionnaire. The deadline for this is likely to be late November 2020. Applicants will be informed of the date once it has been finalised.

Applicants who have met the above criteria will then be ranked for interview on the basis of their GAMSAT scores. There are a limited number of interview places (around 160). Programme coordinators are unable to advise as to the likely GAMSAT scores that will required to be called for interview.

2020 number overview

Total spaces: 55

Total number of applications received: 259

Total number of applicants interviewed: 126

GAMSAT cut-off: overall score of 50, with no subset lower than 49. Paper III was not double weighted.

ScotGEM Admissions are unable to advise as to the likely GAMSAT scores that will be required to be called for interview for 2021 entry.

The interview process

Interviews are usually held at the University of Dundee School of Medicine at Ninewells Hospital. For 2021 entry, all interviews will be conducted online. Further information will be provided to applicants who are invited to interview. 

Interviews will follow the format of multiple mini interviews (MMI). This usually consists of a series of ‘stations’ each lasting seven minutes. You will rotate around the station circuit, going from one station to the next. Each person starts at a different station; it does not matter where you begin as each station is independent of the others.

Each station has a card with simple instructions that are designed to orientate you. Short (30-second) breaks between stations allow you to settle yourself and clear your head for the next station. You can expect to be asked about your motivation to study medicine, relevant work experience and personal attributes, and why you have applied to ScotGEM in particular.

  • Due to the large number of applicants, candidates are expected to accept the date offered for interview. The ScotGEM admissions team will try to offer another date if there is an exceptional reason for a candidate not being able to attend; for example, attending another interview, sitting an examination or due to ill health. However, this cannot always be guaranteed.

    Health reasons

    If a candidate becomes ill near to or on the interview date, they should let the ScotGEM admissions team know as soon as possible by emailing scotgem-admissions@st-andrews.ac.uk. The admissions team will endeavour to offer another date, but will only be able to do so if there are spaces on other interview dates that have been scheduled.

    Mitigating circumstances

    Any mitigating circumstances up to the day of the interview or during the interview that may have affected interview performance should be declared by the end of the interview day. Unfortunately, the admissions team cannot take into account any mitigating circumstances provided after the fact.

Decisions and feedback

Decisions to make offers will be based on the multiple mini interviews. Applicants will be notified of decisions via UCAS. Although some decisions may be made early on in the admissions cycle, the majority of decisions will not be made until mid-March 2021.

The information on UCAS Track will include some feedback as to why an applicant was unsuccessful in their application. The University is unable to provide any further feedback on unsuccessful applications.

Please see the University’s admissions policy for information on the appeal process.
